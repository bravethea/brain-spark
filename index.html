💡 Science-Backed Brain Health Tips

🧠 Brain Training
⚡

Play Quick Flash daily — the ACTIVE trial found speed training cuts dementia risk by 25% over 20 years.

🔁

Booster sessions matter most — the 25% risk reduction only applied to people who returned for follow-up sessions. Consistency is key.

📚

Read every day — reading engages multiple brain networks at once. Fiction is especially good for empathy and social cognition.

🎵

Learn a musical instrument or sing. Music engages more areas of the brain simultaneously than any other activity.

🗣️

Stay socially active — regular conversation and social engagement are among the strongest protective factors against dementia.

✏️

Learn something new — a language, a craft, a recipe. Novelty forces the brain to build new connections.

🏃 Physical Activity
🚶

Walk 30 minutes a day — aerobic exercise increases blood flow to the brain and promotes growth of new brain cells (neurogenesis).

💪

Strength training twice a week — a 2020 Lancet study found resistance exercise significantly reduces cognitive decline risk.

🧘

Try yoga or tai chi — these combine movement with focused attention and have been shown to reduce brain shrinkage in older adults.

💃

Dancing is especially powerful — it combines physical exercise, social interaction, and learning new patterns, all at once.

😴 Sleep & Rest
🌙

Aim for 7–8 hours of sleep. During deep sleep, your brain flushes out toxic waste products including amyloid — a hallmark of Alzheimer's.

🛏️

Keep a regular sleep schedule — going to bed and waking at the same time each day is more protective than just getting enough hours.

📵

No screens 1 hour before bed — blue light suppresses melatonin and disrupts the deep sleep your brain needs to self-clean.

🥦 Diet & Nutrition
🫐

Eat berries regularly — blueberries, strawberries and blackberries are rich in flavonoids linked to slower cognitive decline in large studies.

🐟

Oily fish twice a week — omega-3 fatty acids (in salmon, mackerel, sardines) are essential for brain cell membrane health.

🫒

Follow a Mediterranean-style diet — olive oil, nuts, legumes, vegetables and whole grains. The MIND diet reduces Alzheimer's risk by up to 53%.

🥜

Eat nuts daily — a handful of walnuts in particular has been associated with better memory and brain function in older adults.

🍵

Drink green tea — contains EGCG and L-theanine, which promote calm focus and may protect against plaque build-up in the brain.

💧

Stay hydrated — even mild dehydration impairs concentration and memory. The brain is 75% water. Aim for 6–8 glasses a day.

🧘 Stress & Mental Wellbeing
🌬️

Manage stress actively — chronic stress raises cortisol, which damages the hippocampus (your memory centre). Deep breathing helps immediately.

🧘

Try mindfulness meditation — even 10 minutes a day has been shown in brain scans to increase grey matter density in memory-related regions.

😄

Laugh often — laughter reduces stress hormones and activates multiple brain regions. Humour is genuinely good medicine.

🎨

Creative hobbies protect the brain — painting, knitting, pottery and writing all reduce dementia risk by building cognitive reserve.

🏥 Health Checks
❤️

Keep blood pressure in check — high blood pressure in midlife is one of the biggest modifiable risk factors for dementia. Ask your GP.

🩺

Manage blood sugar — type 2 diabetes significantly increases dementia risk. A healthy diet and regular exercise are the best prevention.

👂

Treat hearing loss early — untreated hearing loss is one of the largest modifiable risk factors for dementia. Get a hearing test if unsure.

🚭

Don't smoke — smoking doubles the risk of dementia by damaging blood vessels and reducing oxygen supply to the brain.

🍷

Limit alcohol — even moderate drinking shrinks brain volume over time. The Lancet Commission on Dementia lists alcohol as a key risk factor.
