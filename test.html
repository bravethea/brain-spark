Cognitive Health Assessment

A comprehensive, science-backed screening tool for memory, language, attention, and behaviour — covering early, mild, and moderate cognitive changes.

⚠️ Important Medical Disclaimer This is a screening tool only — not a clinical diagnosis. Results cannot replace a consultation with a GP, neurologist, or memory specialist. If you have concerns, please speak to a doctor. This tool is based on validated research instruments including the MMSE, MoCA, ACE-III, AD8, and Neuropsychiatric Inventory.
📋 What this test covers
8 domains · ~20 minutes

A — Behavioural & Self-Report Assessment
B — Orientation (time & place)
C — Memory: Immediate Recall
D — Attention & Working Memory
E — Language & Naming
F — Abstract Reasoning
G — Memory: Delayed Recall
H — Visuospatial Perception
Based on MMSE · MoCA · ACE-III · AD8 · NPI

Part A: Behavioural Assessment

Think about how things are compared to 2–3 years ago. Answer as honestly as possible — there are no right or wrong answers.

Adapted from AD8 (Galvin et al., 2005, Neurology) · NPI (Cummings et al., 1994, Neurology) · Lawton IADL Scale (1969)
🗓️

Part B: Orientation

Answer each question from memory — do not check your phone or calendar.

From MMSE (Folstein et al., 1975, J Psychiatric Research) · Max score: 10 points
💭

Part C: Memory — Learning

Study the words carefully. You will be asked to recall them later.

Word list learning adapted from ADAS-Cog (Rosen et al., 1984, Am J Psychiatry) · ACE-III (Hsieh et al., 2013, JNNP)
🎯

Part D: Attention & Working Memory

Two short tasks testing concentration and the ability to hold information in mind.

Serial subtraction from MMSE (Folstein, 1975) · Digit span from Wechsler Memory Scale (Wechsler, 1945)
🗣️

Part E: Language & Naming

Three language tasks: naming objects, word generation, and sentence repetition.

Confrontation naming & verbal fluency from ACE-III (Hsieh et al., 2013) · Boston Naming Test (Kaplan et al., 1983)
🔍

Part F: Abstract Reasoning

Questions about similarities and meaning — testing higher cognitive function.

Similarities task from MoCA (Nasreddine et al., 2005, JAGS) · Abstraction subscale of ACE-III
⏳

Part G: Delayed Recall

Can you remember the words from Part C? This tests long-term memory consolidation.

Delayed free recall is the single strongest predictor of Alzheimer's disease (Sarazin et al., 2007, Archives of Neurology) · Scoring from ACE-III
👁️

Part H: Visuospatial Perception

Tests the brain's ability to interpret and process visual information.

Clock interpretation from CDT (Shulman, 2000, JAGS) · Visuospatial subscale from ACE-III · MoCA visuospatial tasks
